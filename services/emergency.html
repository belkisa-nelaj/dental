What to Do in a Dental Emergency

Quick actions can save your tooth and prevent complications

1

Knocked-Out Tooth

  • Handle by the crown, not the root
  • Rinse gently if dirty (don't scrub)
  • Try to reinsert or keep in milk/saliva
  • See a dentist immediately (within 30 mins ideal)
2

Severe Toothache

  • Rinse with warm salt water
  • Use dental floss to remove any debris
  • Apply cold compress to reduce swelling
  • Avoid aspirin directly on gums
3

Cracked/Broken Tooth

  • Rinse mouth with warm water
  • Save any broken pieces
  • Apply cold compress to reduce swelling
  • Use temporary dental cement if available
4

Lost Filling/Crown

  • Keep the crown if possible
  • Use dental cement or sugarless gum as temporary fix
  • Avoid chewing on that side
  • Don't use super glue

Call Immediately

  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Severe pain that doesn't respond to medication
  • Swelling affecting breathing or swallowing
  • Trauma causing loose or missing teeth

Schedule Soon

  • Dull toothache that comes and goes
  • Lost filling or crown (no pain)
  • Small chip or crack (no pain)
  • Mild sensitivity to hot/cold
